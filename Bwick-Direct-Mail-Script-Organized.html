How the Program Works

IMPORTANT: Slow down — this is the meat and potatoes!
They'll be moving your accounts through a legal process where your creditors are required to provide proof that your debt is valid and legally collectible.

This process must follow a federal and state law called the FDCPA — which stands for the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act.
Once we are off the phone google this law so you can understand your rights.
Now, this law is designed to protect you from 3 things:
  • Predatory lending
  • ILLEGAL collection practices
  • Inaccurate credit reporting
This next step starts by demonstrating to your creditors that you're facing financial hardship.
The only way they will understand this is when you stop making your monthly payments.
Once that happens, the accounts move into collections, which is expected, and this is called a STRATEGIC CHARGE OFF.
Now, here's where everything shifts in your favor.

When a debt is transferred or sold to a collection agency, it's normally missing 3 necessary things:
  • Your date of birth
  • Your Social Security number
  • The original signed contracts
Now remember, the original creditor has all of this information.

But what many people don't realize is there are multiple privacy laws in place that prevent most of your information from being sold or shared with the collection agencies.
For example, your Social Security number can't legally be sold to a collection agency.

And you definitely didn't sign a new contract with the collection agency after the debt was transferred.
So right off the bat, that's 2 out of 3 legal requirements they're missing — and that's where Doc Pros step in.
Their job is to demand that these collection agencies can prove they have the right to legally collect on your debt...

But because they don't have your personal information, they won't be able to provide that proof.
They also send Cease and Desist letters to stop all collection attempts while we work on your behalf.
Now once we show that the debt can't be legally validated, the debt becomes uncollectable.

Which means the collection agency can no longer attempt to collect that debt from you, and you are no longer responsible for the debt.
And to be clear — future lenders won't see any record of the debt ever being in collections.

All the negative remarks are removed from your credit.

So this isn't a band-aid — it's a full reset of your credit, which gives you a clean slate moving forward with a great credit score.
