Cross-Collateralization Script▶
I want to quickly cover something important that protects your money while you're in the program — it's called cross-collateralization.
Since your checking account and credit card are with the same bank, once that card becomes past due, the bank has the right to pull money directly from your account to pay the balance. They don't ask — they just sweep the funds.
Because we're strategically allowing the account to fall behind so the validation process can work, we can't have your income going into a bank that can grab it.
So the safe move is: Open a new checking at a different bank, move direct deposit and bills there, leave the old account mostly empty so there's nothing to sweep.
This simply protects your money while we do our job on the debt.

Debt Settlement vs. Debt Validation
Training Module — Help reps clearly explain how Debt Validation works, why it's legally stronger and financially safer than traditional settlement, and how it protects credit and cash flow better.

1. The Big Picture: Both Are About Getting Out of Debt

Debt Settlement:
Negotiates balances after a charge-off.
You stop paying creditors → accounts go late → credit score tanks.
A negotiator tries to settle accounts for less than owed (usually 40–60%).
You still pay income tax on forgiven amounts.
Credit remains damaged for years.
Debt Validation / Dispute:
Uses federal consumer protection laws (FDCPA, FCRA, FCBA, etc.) to force collectors to prove a debt is valid and legally collectible.
If they cannot produce required documentation, the debt must be deleted or closed — not settled.
Focuses on disputing legality, not negotiating a payoff.
You keep more cash and protect your credit from new damage.

2. Financial Comparison (Average Case)

CategoryDebt SettlementDebt Validation
Total Savings40–50% off balances (but pay taxes on forgiven debt) Plus 25% to the settlement company (after all costs they pay back up to 100% of the debt they owed)Often 60–80% savings, no tax liability
Monthly PaymentHigh, based on negotiated settlementsLow, based on legal service plan
Credit ImpactSevere – late payments and settlements reportedMinimal to moderate – disputed accounts may be removed
Legal ProtectionNone (can still be sued)Strong – uses legal team and dispute process
Timeline36–48 months average6–18 months average depending on accounts

3. Credit Restoration Advantage

Debt Settlement:
Leaves "settled for less than owed" marks.
Score usually drops 100–200 points.
Must rebuild from scratch after program.
Debt Validation:
When debts are invalidated, the negative trade lines are disputed for removal.
Validated debts can be handled legally without settlements.
Goal: restore credit to 680–720+ within program completion.

4. Real-World Example

A client owes $20,000 across five credit cards.

Debt Settlement: pays ~$12,000–$14,000 + taxes → ends up costing ~$15,000 total, also 25% fees to the settlement company it could be up to $20,000 all in!

Debt Validation: legal team disputes each account. Three are deleted, let's say two are verified and negotiated for $4,000 → total ~$6,000-$8,000 total payback.

Savings: $9,000 difference + credit rebound in under a year.

5. Talking Points for Reps

"We don't negotiate your debt — we challenge it legally."
"Our goal isn't to settle — it's to remove what's not legally collectible."
"You keep more money in your pocket and restore your credit faster."
"Debt settlement focuses on paying; we focus on protecting."
"We help you get your financial rights enforced under federal law."

NEPQ Cancellation Saves
Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning — use these to save cancellations
NEPQ Training Video — Jeremy Miner
What is NEPQ? NEPQ stands for Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning — a sales methodology developed by Jeremy Miner. It's a modern, psychology-based selling framework built around asking the right questions rather than pitching or pushing.
Key principles: Ask, don't tell — questions lead prospects to recognize their own pain. Neuro-emotional triggers — tap into emotional drivers behind buying decisions. Disarm defensiveness — avoids high-pressure tactics. Status quo disruption — creates urgency without aggression.
Question framework: 1) Connection questions — Build rapport. 2) Situation questions — Understand where they are now. 3) Problem awareness — Help them articulate pain. 4) Consequence questions — Explore what happens if nothing changes. 5) Solution questions — Guide them to envision a better outcome. 6) Commitment questions — Natural close based on their own reasoning.
1. Revisit Emotional Pain (Current vs. Future State)▶
"Out of curiosity, what was the main reason you enrolled in the first place? Was it more about wanting to stop the creditor pressure, or fixing your credit so you can borrow again?"
Let them respond
"Got it. So if you were to cancel now, do you feel like that would move you closer to the goal you originally had — or back to where things were before?"
2. Problem Awareness + Fear of Future Pain▶
"I get it — making changes like this can feel risky. But just to clarify, if those accounts stay on your credit the way they are now… do you think they'll improve on their own — or possibly get worse over time?"
Let them acknowledge the reality
"Exactly. That's actually why people with lower credit scores benefit the most from this program — it gives you a legal way to clean up your report and finally move forward."
3. Consequences of Doing Nothing▶
If Current:
"Let me ask you something — if nothing changes and those high balances stay where they are for the next 12 to 24 months, what does that mean for your credit and ability to qualify for anything new?"
Let them reflect
"Exactly. That's why people in your position benefit from this kind of legal strategy."
If Behind:
"If nothing changes, and those collections stay on your report for the next 12–24 months, what does that mean for you financially?"
"That's what we're trying to prevent by legally disputing and removing them now, instead of letting the damage drag out."
4. Reframe Cancellation as Losing Progress▶
"You've already taken the first step most people never take — and your file is already in motion. If we stop now, we lose the progress, and you're right back where you started — except now with another delay. Is that something you're willing to risk just yet?"
5. Rebuild Trust + Show Partnership▶
"I totally respect your concern, and that tells me you're serious about your credit. But that's why we're not like traditional debt settlement — we don't negotiate; we use consumer law to challenge the debt altogether. Would it be okay if I walk you through how this actually protects your credit instead of hurting it?"

Debt Type Cheat Sheet & Codes

Quick reference for reading credit report codes and deciding what to enroll.

Credit Report Codes

INST — INSTALLMENT LOAN — Always ask: What is this? What is it SECURED TO? — if anything
OPEN — Treat same as INST — always ask: What is this? What is it SECURED TO?
AUTO — Car must be REPO'd or completely totaled — watch for Joint borrowers attached!
REV — Revolving Credit Card / Line of Credit — always good but watch for Authorized User!
COLL — Collection account — always good
MTG / EDU — Mortgage and Student Loan — DON'T enroll EVER

Borrower Type Codes

B | B — Borrower / Borrower = just the client's debt. Good to go.
J | B — JOINT Borrower = there is a joint borrower attached to the debt. "Who is it?" Spouse or family member — they must be on board for the "Storm" (their credit will be affected too!). If joint borrower is on board, include their Name + Number + DOB in Co-Applicant Section. ONLY if the account is already IN Collections — no worries if client doesn't want to include them.
If spouse/family member wants to be included — ask if they have any Personal Debt. Include it to increase fees — and they also get the CREDIT UPDATE step included for FREE!
A | B — Client is just an AUTHORIZED USER on this credit card — do NOT include, not theirs!

Installment Loan Rules

INST on Credit Report — "WHAT IS THIS DEBT?" "WHAT IS IT SECURED TO?" A lot of these ARE secured — to a car, home improvement loan, tractor, etc. — we cannot include. But SOME are unsecured. Always ask: WHAT IS IT / SECURED TO?

Credit Union Rules

A) A credit card from a credit union is GOOD — ALL DAY, RUN IT
B) A personal loan or installment loan from a credit union is BAD — Exclude

Secured vs Unsecured

You ALWAYS HAVE TO ASK — don't rely on what it says on the credit report. It is sometimes listed as UNSECURED but is actually SECURED. You have to ask! Never ever try to push through a debt that is secured to an asset — client will be SUED, ASSET taken, and client will be pissed off you lied to them.
Do NOT Enroll "CHIME" or Secured REV's or Credit Builders
