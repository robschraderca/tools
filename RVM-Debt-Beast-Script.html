Explaining "Validate"

Okay, so I wanted to take a moment to explain that first word "Validate" because this is going to do the most work for you with the types of debt you have — which all have a few things in common. They are private, unsecured, not tied to collateral.
In order to properly explain validation, I just want to touch on two laws that we're using which you could kind of consider the foundation of the program — specifically the Fair Credit Reporting Act and the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act.
These laws outline what a creditor can and cannot do, and how they must follow the laws in order to collect a debt from a borrower.
Now that being said, these laws at their core functions are simply designed to prevent false and inaccurate reporting, but these also are the exact laws as far as what sort of requirements a company needs to carry to even prove the debt's owed to begin with.
The way they prove it is through triple-verified debt by having your DOB, Social, and the original signed contract — which obviously nothing out of the normal here. It's the exact information that was used to open up these trade lines to begin with.
But a lot of people don't know is when a debt is sold or transferred in any way, there's multiple privacy laws in place that filter most of this information out from transferring.
