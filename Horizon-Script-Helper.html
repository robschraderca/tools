Debt Settlement vs. Debt Validation
Training Module — Help reps clearly explain how Debt Validation works, why it's legally stronger and financially safer than traditional settlement, and how it protects credit and cash flow better.

1. The Big Picture: Both Are About Getting Out of Debt

Debt Settlement:
Negotiates balances after a charge-off.
You stop paying creditors → accounts go late → credit score tanks.
A negotiator tries to settle accounts for less than owed (usually 40–60%).
You still pay income tax on forgiven amounts.
Credit remains damaged for years.
Debt Validation / Dispute:
Uses federal consumer protection laws (FDCPA, FCRA, FCBA, etc.) to force collectors to prove a debt is valid and legally collectible.
If they cannot produce required documentation, the debt must be deleted or closed — not settled.
Focuses on disputing legality, not negotiating a payoff.
You keep more cash and protect your credit from new damage.

What is NEPQ? NEPQ stands for Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning — a sales methodology developed by Jeremy Miner. It's a modern, psychology-based selling framework built around asking the right questions rather than pitching or pushing.
Key principles: Ask, don't tell — questions lead prospects to recognize their own pain. Neuro-emotional triggers — tap into emotional drivers behind buying decisions. Disarm defensiveness — avoids high-pressure tactics. Status quo disruption — creates urgency without aggression.
Question framework: 1) Connection questions — Build rapport. 2) Situation questions — Understand where they are now. 3) Problem awareness — Help them articulate pain. 4) Consequence questions — Explore what happens if nothing changes. 5) Solution questions — Guide them to envision a better outcome. 6) Commitment questions — Natural close based on their own reasoning.

Debt Type Cheat Sheet & Codes

Quick reference for reading credit report codes and deciding what to enroll.

Credit Report Codes

INST — INSTALLMENT LOAN — Always ask: What is this? What is it SECURED TO? — if anything
OPEN — Treat same as INST — always ask: What is this? What is it SECURED TO?
AUTO — Car must be REPO'd or completely totaled — watch for Joint borrowers attached!
REV — Revolving Credit Card / Line of Credit — always good but watch for Authorized User!
COLL — Collection account — always good
MTG / EDU — Mortgage and Student Loan — DON'T enroll EVER

Borrower Type Codes

B | B — Borrower / Borrower = just the client's debt. Good to go.
J | B — JOINT Borrower = there is a joint borrower attached to the debt. "Who is it?" Spouse or family member — they must be on board for the "Storm" (their credit will be affected too!). If joint borrower is on board, include their Name + Number + DOB in Co-Applicant Section. ONLY if the account is already IN Collections — no worries if client doesn't want to include them.
If spouse/family member wants to be included — ask if they have any Personal Debt. Include it to increase fees — and they also get the CREDIT UPDATE step included for FREE!
A | B — Client is just an AUTHORIZED USER on this credit card — do NOT include, not theirs!

Installment Loan Rules

INST on Credit Report — "WHAT IS THIS DEBT?" "WHAT IS IT SECURED TO?" A lot of these ARE secured — to a car, home improvement loan, tractor, etc. — we cannot include. But SOME are unsecured. Always ask: WHAT IS IT / SECURED TO?

Credit Union Rules

A) A credit card from a credit union is GOOD — ALL DAY, RUN IT
B) A personal loan or installment loan from a credit union is BAD — Exclude

Secured vs Unsecured

You ALWAYS HAVE TO ASK — don't rely on what it says on the credit report. It is sometimes listed as UNSECURED but is actually SECURED. You have to ask! Never ever try to push through a debt that is secured to an asset — client will be SUED, ASSET taken, and client will be pissed off you lied to them.
Do NOT Enroll "CHIME" or Secured REV's or Credit Builders
